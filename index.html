Why this layer is missing

The internet was never designed to prove what’s real.

E-commerce made global trade frictionless — and just-in-time. But the “web of trust” was never designed for what matters most: provable authenticity of physical goods, and integrity of customs & trade data.

  • Counterfeit goods cross borders at scale, with little shared intelligence.
  • Under-valuation and false declarations erode customs revenues.
  • Each marketplace keeps its own siloed logs — there is no global authenticity log.
  • Consumers and regulators see only a “front-end store,” not the truth of the product.

What is Showplly

Every product becomes a verifiable digital identity.

Showplly is an authenticity infrastructure. We give each physical product its own NFT-based Digital Product ID (DPID), anchored on Ethereum and mapped into Oracle’s systems.

Once a product has a DPID, its authenticity, origin, and customs data can be linked to any marketplace, carrier, or customs system — without redesigning those systems.

  • DPID = Series-level NFT Digital Product ID: One DPID per product series, linked to brand, model, specs, and verified metadata.
  • Web2.5 commerce first: Consumers and merchants stay in a familiar Web2 environment. Wallets and keys are abstracted away. DPID is invisible to end-users — but verifiable by systems.
  • Oracle-native: DPID registries and metadata can be mirrored into Oracle databases, ledgers, and analytics tools. Perfect fit for a hybrid Web2/Web3 stack.

Pre-RWA identification layer

Before tokenization, the world needs identification.

Showplly is not a financialization project first. It is a truth project. Before a physical good can be part of a safe RWA structure, we need a robust way to say “this is the thing”.

  • DPID is not a security. It is an NFT-based Digital Product ID — a series-level identifier for references, verification, and logistics.
  • Regulatory-light first, finance-ready later. By focusing on product identification and customs data integrity, Showplly can deploy within existing commercial and customs frameworks.
  • Oracle as the neutral backbone. Oracle’s infrastructure can safely host DPID registries, customs data integrations, and analytics while remaining agnostic to any eventual RWA designs.

Technology
