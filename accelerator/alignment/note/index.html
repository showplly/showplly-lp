Architecture Note– Pre-Financial Identity Infrastructure
Pre-financial identity layer

Showplly is not a marketplace. It is identity infrastructure.

Showplly provides a shared, authenticity-first identity layer for physical goods — the trust foundation that future marketplaces, RWA platforms, and AI agents can rely on.

Before tokenization, automation, or financial settlement, the world needs a consistent way to identify “what is real.” Showplly sits in the missing middle: it anchors product identity, pre-financial identity, and provenance/ownership graphs so higher-level systems can execute safely.

Read the Architecture Note (2–3 minutes)

The Three-Layer Stack

1) AI Agents / Web3 Applications (Execution Layer)

At the top of the stack are autonomous agents, Web3 applications, and execution systems that act on behalf of users and institutions. These systems can only operate safely when they can verify “what the object is” and “what happened to it” using shared, tamper-evident identity and provenance signals.

  • Agents need reliable identity to avoid fraud, misrouting, and counterfeit interactions.
  • Protocols need consistent object identity to reason about ownership, rights, and eligibility.

2) Showplly Identity Layer (Pre-Financial Core Layer)

Showplly occupies the missing middle layer. It is not a marketplace and not a payment rail. It is a pre-financial identity infrastructure that anchors product identity, provenance, and ownership graphs in a way that higher layers can reuse across ecosystems.

  • Product identity anchor: a stable identity for physical items and their representations.
  • Pre-financial identity anchor: identity established before settlement/custody/token issuance.
  • Native provenance / ownership graph: a history of verifications and lifecycle events that can be audited.

3) Physical Goods Layer (Reality Layer)

At the bottom is the real world: factories, logistics, cross-border shipping, and distribution. This is where fraud, mis-declaration, and authenticity disputes occur. Showplly’s role is to provide an identity and provenance foundation that can be shared across stakeholders without forcing end-users into Web3 complexity.
